Raccoon vs. the Rat

A dark-fantasy arena game where a raccoon defends its trash from a giant rat and an invading army of cockroaches.

Engine Unity and C#
Genre Third-person action and arena defence
Main systems Combat, AI, animation, pooling and game-state management

Project overview

The player controls a raccoon defending a trash container inside a dark arena inspired by Mordor.

A giant rat moves laterally across the battlefield and repeatedly releases cockroaches that advance towards the player's trash.

The raccoon can move, run and fire projectiles while trying to stop the incoming enemies and defeat the rat boss.

Gameplay demonstration showing combat, cockroach waves, the Ring ability and the final victory and defeat states.

1. Core gameplay loop

The game is structured around a continuous defence loop. The boss generates enemies, the player intercepts them and the arena becomes increasingly difficult to control.

STEP 01

Rat movement

The boss patrols laterally, changes direction and occasionally pauses its movement.

STEP 02

Enemy spawning

Cockroaches are taken from a reusable pool and released into the arena.

STEP 03

Player defence

The raccoon moves around the arena and fires projectiles at the boss and its minions.

STEP 04

Win or lose

The game ends when either the rat or the raccoon reaches zero health.

2. Player movement and combat

Player input is handled through Unity's Input System. The raccoon supports directional movement, running and ranged attacks.

MOVEMENT

Third-person controls

The character moves inside a limited arena using a CharacterController and separate walking and running speeds.

ATTACK

Fireball projectiles

The raccoon launches fireballs from a dedicated firing point towards the enemy target.

DAMAGE

Health and reaction

Damage updates the player's health, triggers a reaction animation and activates a short invulnerability window.

ARENA CONTROL

Movement boundaries

The player is constrained to the playable area to keep the combat inside the intended arena.

3. Rat boss behaviour

The giant rat acts as the central boss and coordinates the pressure applied to the player.

It moves from side to side, changes direction, pauses at irregular intervals and generates new cockroaches during combat.

MOVEMENT

Lateral patrol

The rat travels between two limits and rotates its model according to its current movement direction.

SPAWNING

Cockroach waves

Enemies are requested from the pool at regular intervals and released from a dedicated spawn point.

ANIMATION

Dance, damage and death

The boss includes an introductory dance, movement, damage reaction and death animation.

BOSS HEALTH

Victory condition

Fireballs reduce the rat's health. Reaching zero health triggers the game's victory state.

4. Cockroach enemy system

Cockroaches form the secondary enemy type. They move in a fixed direction towards the player's trash and damage the raccoon when they reach their target area.

Instead of being continuously instantiated and destroyed, they are managed through a queue-based object pool.

Object pooling cycle

INITIALISATION

Pre-created enemies

A fixed number of cockroaches is instantiated when the game begins and stored as inactive objects.

SPAWN

Reuse from queue

The rat obtains the next available enemy from the pool instead of creating a new instance.

GAMEPLAY

Movement and damage

Active enemies move towards the target, receive damage and trigger player damage when reaching the defence zone.

RETURN

Recycle instance

Dead, expired or successful cockroaches are disabled and returned to the pool for later reuse.

5. The Ring ability

The raccoon has access to an additional ability inspired by the One Ring.

When activated, the character becomes temporarily invisible and invulnerable. The player can continue attacking while the rat pauses the generation of new cockroaches.

VISIBILITY

Temporary invisibility

The raccoon model disappears while the ability remains active.

DEFENCE

Invulnerability

Incoming damage is ignored during the active ability window.

OFFENCE

Continued attacks

The player can still fire projectiles while hidden.

ENEMY RESPONSE

Spawn interruption

The rat temporarily stops releasing new cockroaches.

6. Animation system

Multiple animated elements are coordinated through Unity's Animator system.

The raccoon uses a Blend Tree for locomotion and a separate Animation Layer for damage reactions.

Element Animation behaviour
Raccoon Idle, walking, running and damage reactions using a Blend Tree and Animation Layer.
Giant rat Introductory dance, lateral movement, damage and death.
Cockroaches Continuous locomotion while advancing towards the target.
Tower eye Search movement during gameplay and target tracking during defeat.
Ring Appearance, rotation, movement and scale reduction.

7. Game architecture

The project applies several common game-development patterns to separate responsibilities and reduce unnecessary runtime allocation.

SINGLETON

GameManager

Centralises the global game state, victory, defeat and scene restart.

OBSERVER

Subject and Observer

Gameplay events are communicated without tightly coupling the sender to the receiving systems.

DATA POOLING

Cockroach pool

Enemies are reused through a queue instead of repeatedly creating and destroying GameObjects.

LEVEL OF DETAIL

Tower LOD

The distant tower switches to a simplified silhouette while keeping the eye and lighting effects active.

8. Visual and audio presentation

The scene combines a dark-fantasy art direction with custom lighting, music, camera work and gameplay feedback.

CINEMACHINE

Camera introduction

The game begins with an introductory camera movement before switching to the gameplay camera.

SHADER GRAPH

Tower light cone

A custom animated material combines noise, transparency and red-orange colour variation.

PARTICLE POOL

Impact effects

Particle systems are reused for fireball impacts against cockroaches.

AUDIO

Music and sound effects

A persistent AudioManager controls looping music and tagged gameplay sound effects.

9. Victory and defeat

The game has two clear final states.

Defeating the giant rat triggers victory. Losing all raccoon health triggers defeat, after which the tower eye locks onto the player and attacks with its cone of light.

VICTORY

Boss defeated

The rat's death notifies the game-state system and activates the final victory interface.

DEFEAT

The eye strikes

The tower stops searching, aims directly at the raccoon and intensifies its light cone during the defeat sequence.

Technical value

This project combines player control, ranged combat, enemy behaviour, animation systems, pooling, event-driven architecture, LOD, Shader Graph, particles, audio and complete win-and-lose gameplay states inside a single Unity project.
